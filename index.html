Trendy

The Skater Girl

The skater style, which is also known as streetwear is basically casual and comfortable clothing, like t-shirts, jeans, baseball caps, and sneakers. This type of style was inspired by surf culture in Los Angeles in the 1970s and 1980s. As of right now some of the popular brands for this style is Supreme and Stussy. Business Casual

This is a style that is less formal than traditional business wear but it gives off a business-like impression. You would usually go for this style if you work in a corporate setting or if you’re going for a job interview. This style was introduced in 1966 when the Hawaiian garment industry was trying to sell shirts.The industry managed to come up with a deal with Hawaiian businesses called Aloha Fridays, which allowed business employees to wear their Hawaiian shirts to work once a week. Eventually, the concept of combining casual clothing with regular business attire became a part of mainstream society. (Click the image to find out more!)


Preppy

The preppy style is associated with old private preparatory schools located in the Northeast. This style is synonymous with school uniforms, but the difference is that they give off an aura of intelligence, etiquette, and an upper-class upbringing, but that does not necessarily mean that you have to be upper class to dress this way. This is style emerged in the 1960s by female students who were a part of the sister colleges of the Ivy Leagues. (Click the image to find out more!)


Old School

The old school style mostly refers to clothing from the 1960s to 1980s. In the 60s the type of clothing that became popular were miniskirts, floral printed dresses that were more flowy, chain link belts, and disc-like earrings. In the 70s dresses with ruffles and crazy patterns that were up to the knee became popular. The shoes that were worn in that decade were platform shoes. In the 80s, fitness became part of mainstream society, so neon spandex, a body suit with an off shoulder sweater paired with a headband would be a common sight. (Click the image to find out more!)

They aren't really goth and not fully punk but this is their inbetween. Fashion wise its a mix between Johnny Rotten and the people who hung out in the bat cave. If you want to dress like one don’t shop at hot topic. They make their own clothes on the go and borrow their friends or moms boots/baggy clothes. Boho

Bohemian fashion is usually associated with artists, writers, and intellectuals. This style consists of loose and colorful clothing and does not include wearing restrictive garments like bras or corsets. The clothing is paired with loose flowing hair and colorful scarves that are worn at the head or neck or as a substitute for belts. Other pieces of clothing includes tunics, loose trousers, and sandals. This fashion style originated in France as a counterculture movement during the time of the French Revolution. Hipster

Hipster fashion gives off an “I don't care” vibe. Most outfits consist of denim with a few rips. Other pieces of clothing include vintage skirts, jumpsuits, leggings that are paired with flannel shirts, graphic t-shirts, and oversized sweatshirts or cardigans. The accessories that go along with this style are oversized glasses, slouchy beanies, and boots. Most hipster hairstyles comprise of messy waves and topknots. (Click the image to find out more!)


E-Girls

This style comprises of wearing dark colored, pastel colored, or checkered clothing. The hairstyle associated with this is short hair with bangs and it is sometimes dyed. People who do follow this style have face piercings too. In 2019, E-Girls are known to have blush on their nose and cheeks and have hearts drawn underneath their eyes. Vintage Chic

Vintage chic is a classy, modest, and sophisticated style. The cloth type for this style consists of cotton, linen, tweed, wool, and silk. The clothing for vintage chic has bold patterns with neutral colors such as black, white, navy blue, cream, grey, and emerald green.Dresses must also be fitted to your figure but it should not have hemlines exceeding a couple of inches above your knee. For events in the evening, the dresses should be floor length. The clothes are paired with simple jewelry. (Click the image to find out more!)
